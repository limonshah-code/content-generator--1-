## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 1.6 Cu Ft Built In Microwave

Choosing the right microwave involves more than just picking a color. Consider these key factors to ensure your new appliance meets all your kitchen needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, determine the type of microwave you need. While many 1.6 cu. ft. models are designed for the countertop, some can be installed into cabinetry with a compatible trim kit for a built-in look. Over-the-range (OTR) models are another option, combining a microwave and a range hood to save counter space. Consider your kitchen layout, available space, and whether you need ventilation features. Think about your daily use: do you primarily reheat coffee, cook elaborate meals, or defrost large items? Your answer will guide you toward the right feature set.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
The price for a 1.6 cu. ft. microwave can vary significantly. Basic, reliable countertop models can be found in the $150-$200 range. Mid-range options with advanced features like sensor cooking or inverter technology typically fall between $200 and $300. True built-in models or over-the-range units will be at the higher end, often costing $300-$500 or more, especially when you factor in the cost of a trim kit or professional installation. Set a realistic budget based on the features that are most important to you.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that will simplify your life. **Sensor cooking** is a high-value feature that automatically adjusts cooking time and power. **Inverter technology** provides more even cooking and defrosting. Check the **wattage**—higher wattage (1100W or more) generally means faster cooking. One-touch buttons for common items like popcorn, potatoes, and beverages add convenience. Also, consider features like a mute function, a child lock for safety, and multi-stage cooking for more complex recipes.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
A microwave is a long-term investment, so durability is key. Look for models with a sturdy build and high-quality materials, such as a stainless steel finish which is both durable and easy to clean. Check the warranty details. A standard one-year warranty on parts and labor is common, but some brands offer extended warranties on critical components like the magnetron, which is a good sign of durability. Reading user reviews can also provide insight into the long-term reliability of a specific model.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with well-known and reputable brands like GE, Toshiba, Frigidaire, and Farberware often pays off. These companies have a long history of manufacturing reliable appliances and typically offer better customer service and easier access to replacement parts. While a lesser-known brand might offer a lower price, an established brand often provides more peace of mind and a more dependable product.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Even within the 1.6 cu. ft. category, external dimensions can vary. Before you buy, measure the space where you plan to place the microwave—width, depth, and height. For countertop models, ensure there's enough clearance around the unit for proper ventilation (usually a few inches on the top, back, and sides). For built-in or OTR models, your measurements must be precise to match the required cabinet cutout dimensions.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy 1.6 cu ft built in microwave?

Investing in a 1.6 cu. ft. microwave is a wise decision for a wide range of households. This size hits the perfect middle ground, offering a significant capacity advantage over smaller 0.9 or 1.1 cu. ft. models without the massive footprint of a 2.0+ cu. ft. unit. It's large enough to comfortably fit a standard dinner plate, a large casserole dish, or family-sized portions, making it highly versatile for daily use.

Compared to smaller alternatives, a 1.6 cu. ft. model often comes with more power and a more advanced feature set, such as sensor cooking and larger turntables, which contribute to better and more even cooking results. While it may cost slightly more than a compact microwave, the long-term benefits are substantial. You won't find yourself struggling to fit certain dishes or wishing for more power.

For those considering a built-in or over-the-range setup, this size integrates beautifully into standard cabinetry, providing a high-end, streamlined look that can increase your kitchen's functionality and aesthetic appeal. The investment pays off in daily convenience, cooking flexibility, and an appliance that can grow with your family's needs.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose 1.6 cu ft built in microwave?

Choosing the best 1.6 cu. ft. microwave involves a methodical approach to ensure it fits your space, budget, and lifestyle. Follow these steps for a successful purchase.

1.  **Measure Your Space First:** The most critical step is to measure the intended location. For a countertop model, check width, depth, and height, leaving room for ventilation. For a built-in or OTR model, measure the cabinet cutout precisely. An appliance that doesn't fit is a costly mistake.

2.  **Define Your Primary Use:** Be honest about how you'll use the microwave. If you're mainly reheating and making popcorn, a basic, reliable model will suffice. If you plan to cook vegetables, defrost meat regularly, or prepare multi-step recipes, look for advanced features like sensor cooking, inverter technology, and programmable stages.

3.  **Set Your Budget:** Determine your price range. Remember to factor in the potential extra cost of a trim kit for built-in models or professional installation for OTR units. This will help you narrow down your options effectively.

4.  **Compare Key Features:** Create a shortlist of 2-3 models in your budget. Compare their wattage, control panels, pre-set functions, and any unique features like a position-memory turntable or mute function. Prioritize the features that align with your primary use.

5.  **Read Reviews and Check Warranties:** Look at recent user reviews to gauge real-world performance and reliability. Pay attention to comments about noise level, ease of use, and customer service. Finally, check the warranty details, especially for the magnetron, to ensure you're making a durable investment.

**Common Mistake to Avoid:** A common mistake is focusing only on wattage or brand name. A high-wattage microwave with a poor design will still cook unevenly. Similarly, even great brands have different tiers of products. Focus on the combination of features and user feedback that best suits your specific needs.

### How do I maintain my 1.6 cu ft built in microwave?
To maintain your microwave, wipe up spills immediately with a damp cloth. Keep the exterior clean and ensure the vents are not blocked to allow for proper air circulation.
