## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Boys Drawstring Novelty Swimwear

Choosing the right pair of swim trunks is about more than just picking a cool pattern. The best swimwear combines comfort, durability, and safety. Here’s a guide to help you navigate the key factors.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, consider how the swim trunks will be used. Is your child a casual swimmer at the local pool, or an aspiring surfer who spends hours in saltwater and sun? For intense activity, look for 4-way stretch fabrics and supportive liners (like compression liners) to allow for maximum movement and prevent chafing. For casual use, a standard polyester trunk with a mesh liner may be perfectly sufficient. Also, think about versatility. Some board shorts are stylish enough to double as casual shorts for the boardwalk or a picnic.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Boys' swim trunks come in a wide price range. You can find basic, functional pairs for as little as $15-$20. Mid-range options, often with better materials, more durable prints, and features like UPF protection, typically fall between $20 and $35. Premium brands, like Volcom, can cost $40 or more, but you're often paying for superior construction, brand reputation, and advanced fabric technologies. Set a budget based on your needs, but be prepared to invest a little more for features like excellent sun protection and chafe-free liners, which can significantly improve the wearing experience.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **UPF Rating:** This is one of the most important safety features. Look for a UPF (Ultraviolet Protection Factor) of 50+, which blocks over 98% of the sun's harmful UV rays.
- **Liner Type:** The two main types are traditional mesh and modern compression (boxer brief) liners. Mesh is standard and breathable, but can sometimes cause chafing. Compression liners offer more support, prevent chafing, and keep sand out, making them a popular upgrade.
- **Fabric:** Quick-dry polyester is the standard. Blends with spandex offer added stretch and mobility. Look for fabrics that are chlorine-resistant and salt-resistant to prevent fading and degradation.
- **Closure:** An elastic waistband with a functional drawstring is essential for a secure, adjustable fit that won't slip down during active play.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Inspect the stitching. Double-stitching or flatlock seams are signs of good quality and help prevent irritation. The material should feel substantial, not flimsy. For printed trunks, check if the pattern is part of the fabric (dye-sublimated) rather than a surface print, as this will prevent cracking and peeling. Reinforced grommets for the drawstring and pocket drainage also indicate better construction.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While you can find great trunks from newer brands, established manufacturers like Volcom or trusted children's brands like RUGGEDBUTTS often have a proven track record for quality, safety standards, and customer service. They typically invest more in material research and construction techniques. Reading reviews can give you insight into a brand's consistency and durability over time.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
For swimwear, this is less about storage at home and more about packing for a trip. Lightweight, quick-drying fabrics are ideal for travel as they take up little space and can be washed and dried overnight. Trunks that can be rolled up tightly without wrinkling are also a plus. Ensure you check the brand's specific size chart, as sizing can vary significantly between manufacturers.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose boys drawstring novelty swimwear?

Choosing the best boys' swimwear involves a few simple steps to ensure you get the perfect combination of style, comfort, and function. Following this guide will help you avoid common pitfalls and make a choice you and your child will be happy with.

**Step 1: Prioritize Sun Safety**
Before looking at any other feature, check for a UPF 50+ rating. This is your first line of defense against sunburn and long-term skin damage. Make this a non-negotiable feature.

**Step 2: Determine the Right Liner**
Discuss with your child what they find comfortable. If they have experienced chafing with traditional mesh liners, strongly consider trunks with a modern compression or boxer brief liner. For toddlers or those with very sensitive skin, a liner-less board short paired with a separate swim brief could also be an option.

**Step 3: Evaluate the Fabric**
For active kids, a polyester-spandex blend with 4-way stretch is ideal as it allows for a full range of motion. If the primary use is casual swimming, 100% quick-dry polyester is a great, durable choice. Always opt for quick-drying materials to enhance comfort out of the water.

**Step 4: Check for a Secure Fit**
Look for an elastic waistband paired with a functional, adjustable drawstring. This ensures the trunks will stay securely in place during cannonballs and dives, and it allows for some room to grow. Avoid purely elastic waists, as they can stretch out over time.

**Step 5: Let Them Choose the Style**
Once you've narrowed down the options based on safety and function, let your child pick the pattern or color. Whether it's dinosaurs, sharks, palm trees, or a cool geometric print, allowing them to choose the 'novelty' aspect ensures they will be excited to wear their new swim trunks.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- **Ignoring the Size Chart:** Don't just buy their regular pants size. Swimwear brands vary, so always measure their waist and compare it to the manufacturer's specific size chart.
- **Sacrificing Quality for Price:** The cheapest option might seem tempting, but it will likely fade, stretch out, or cause discomfort, leading to you buying another pair anyway.
- **Forgetting Pockets:** Pockets are incredibly useful for carrying shells, goggles, or small toys. Look for at least one pocket, preferably with a drainage hole.
