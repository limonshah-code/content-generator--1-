## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Jiawei Technology All Light Bulbs

Choosing the right light bulb goes beyond just screwing it in. To get the best performance, efficiency, and longevity, you need to consider a few key factors. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to make a smart purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you buy, think about where and how the bulb will be used. Do you need bright, focused light for a kitchen counter or soft, ambient light for a living room? Check the base type (e.g., E26 is the standard screw-in base) to ensure it fits your fixture. For recessed lighting, measure the can diameter (e.g., 4, 5, or 6 inches). If you plan to use a dimmer, you must select a bulb that is explicitly labeled as 'dimmable'.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($10-$30 ranges)
LED light bulbs have a higher upfront cost than old incandescent bulbs, but they save you money in the long run through lower energy bills and a much longer lifespan. A single, high-quality standard bulb can range from $5 to $15. Multi-packs often offer better value, potentially bringing the per-bulb cost down. Specialized bulbs like large floodlights or all-in-one retrofit kits can range from $15 to $30 or more.
