## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Vacuum For Hair Salon

Choosing the right vacuum is crucial for maintaining a clean and professional salon. Here are the key factors to consider to ensure you make the best investment for your business.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess your salon's specific needs. Do you need a stationary, touchless vacuum for quick sweeps between clients, or a versatile upright model for deep cleaning floors, upholstery, and stairs? A touchless vacuum like the EyeVac Pro is perfect for constant, quick cleanups of hair on hard floors. An upright vacuum like the Shark Navigator offers more flexibility for a complete, top-to-bottom clean. Consider your flooring types (tile, wood, low-pile carpet) and any additional cleaning tasks you might have.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Salon vacuums range in price, and it's important to set a realistic budget. Entry-level touchless models like the Gearwiz can be found for around $100-$150. Industry-standard models like the EyeVac Pro typically fall in the $150-$200 range. ## What Is the Best Way to Choose vacuum for hair salon?

Choosing the best vacuum for your hair salon involves a methodical approach to ensure it meets all your needs. Follow these steps for a successful purchase.

If your main goal is to eliminate the dustpan for quick, constant hair sweeps on a hard floor, a touchless model is your best bet. If you need a single machine for deep cleaning carpets, furniture, and floors, an upright is more suitable.

2.  **Define Your Space:** Measure the area where a stationary vacuum would be placed. For uprights, consider your storage options. The layout of your salon is also important; a salon with many tight corners and obstacles will benefit greatly from an upright with swivel steering.

3.  **Set Your Budget:** Determine how much you are willing to invest. Remember that this is a business tool. Spending a bit more for a durable, powerful, and warrantied machine can save you money on replacements and repairs in the long run.

4.  **Prioritize Key Features:** Make a list of must-have features. For most salons, these will be strong suction (high wattage), excellent filtration (HEPA or multi-stage), and ease of use (automatic sensors, lightweight design).

5.  **Read Reviews and Compare Models:** Look at our detailed reviews and compare the pros and cons of the top contenders. Pay close attention to user experiences from other salon or pet grooming professionals, as their needs will be similar to yours.
