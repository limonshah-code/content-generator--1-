## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 3 Tray Air Fryer

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before clicking buy, determine if you are looking for a standalone appliance with three racks or replacement trays for your current unit. Standalone 3-tray ovens are better for large families and dehydrating. If you already own a 10QT oven, ensure the replacement trays match your specific model dimensions to maintain proper airflow and safety.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($15-$150 ranges)
Air fryer accessories like silicone liners or replacement trays usually range from $15 to $30. If you are looking for a full 3-tray countertop oven, expect to spend between $80 and $150. Investing a bit more in a well-known brand like Chefman or Instant Pot often pays off in durability and better customer support.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for high wattage (1500W-1700W) to ensure the unit can heat all three trays evenly. For accessories, ensure they are made of food-grade stainless steel or BPA-free silicone. Features like non-stick coatings are convenient but check that they are PFOA-free to avoid toxic fumes at high temperatures.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Heavier stainless steel frames (like 6mm thickness) are less likely to warp or pop in the oven. For trays, 304-grade stainless steel is the gold standard for rust resistance. Check user reviews specifically for reports of non-stick coatings flaking off, as this is a common failure point in lower-quality products.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Brands like Chefman, Nuwave, and Instant Pot have established safety records and standardized sizing. Buying from reputable brands ensures that replacement parts will be available in the future. Third-party accessory brands like GXF or Kispog are often reliable if they specialize in specific model fits.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
A 10L multi-tray oven is quite large and requires dedicated counter space. Measure your cabinets before purchasing. If space is at a premium, a compact 4QT unit with stackable accessories might be a better choice than a full oven-style air fryer.
