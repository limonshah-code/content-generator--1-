## What to Look for Before Buying the Best 30 Inch Convection Microwave

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you make a purchase, determine if you need an over-the-range (OTR) or a built-in model. OTR models are great for saving counter space and providing ventilation for your cooktop. Built-in models provide a cleaner, integrated look and are often placed at eye level or below the counter. Consider who will be using it; a drawer-style microwave is excellent for accessibility, while an OTR model keeps it out of reach of small children.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($300-$1,200)
Prices for a 30-inch convection microwave vary wildly. Entry-level over-the-range models typically start around $300 to $500. These offer basic convection features and decent ventilation. Mid-range units between $500 and $800 often include sensor cooking and air fry modes. Premium built-in or drawer models can range from $800 to $1,200+, offering high-end finishes, advanced technology, and professional-grade performance.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for wattage; higher wattage (900W-1100W) means faster cooking. Convection capability is the main draw, so check if it has a heating element or just a fan. Air frying is a popular modern addition that adds value. Sensor cooking is essential for those who want the machine to do the work of timing. Regularly wipe down the interior with a damp cloth and mild soap. If it has a stainless steel interior, avoid abrasive scrubbers. For OTR models, clean the grease filters monthly in the dishwasher or with warm soapy water to ensure the ventilation system remains effective.
