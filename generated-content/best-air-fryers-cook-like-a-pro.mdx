## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Air Fryers Cook Like A Pro

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you buy, think about what you'll cook most often. Are you a single person looking for quick, crispy snacks, or a family of five needing to roast a whole chicken? A small, 4-quart basket-style air fryer might be perfect for one or two people, while a larger, 10-quart-plus oven-style model is better for bigger meals and offers more versatility, like baking and toasting. Consider functions like dehydrating for making jerky or rotisserie for flavorful meats. Match the appliance's capabilities to your culinary lifestyle.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Air fryer prices can range widely, from around $50 to over $400. Basic models under $100 will handle core air frying tasks well but may lack durability and advanced features. Mid-range options ($100-$200) often offer larger capacities, digital controls, and more presets. Premium models ($200+) typically provide superior build quality, more functions (like grilling or steaming), smart technology, and more precise temperature control.
