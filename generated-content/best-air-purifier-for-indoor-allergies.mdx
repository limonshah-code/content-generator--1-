## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Air Purifier For Indoor Allergies

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before making a purchase, identify the specific allergens you are targeting. If you have pets, look for units with specialized pet modes or U-shaped inlets. For those sensitive to seasonal pollen, a unit with a high CADR for pollen is essential. Consider whether you need a dedicated bedroom unit with silent operation or a high-capacity model for an open-concept living area.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($100-$500 ranges)
Air purifiers range significantly in price. Entry-level models for small rooms usually fall between $50 and $100. Mid-range units for standard living areas with smart features typically cost $150 to $250. Premium models for massive spaces or medical-grade filtration can exceed $400. Always factor in the long-term cost of replacement filters, which usually need changing every 6 to 12 months.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for True HEPA filtration (H13 grade is preferred) as it captures 99.97 percent of particles. CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) is the most important metric for speed. Additionally, smart sensors that adjust fan speed automatically can save energy and ensure you are always breathing clean air. App connectivity is a bonus for those who want to track air quality over time.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Check for certifications like AHAM Verifide, Energy Star, and CARB. These indicate that the unit has been independently tested for safety and performance. A brushless motor is often a sign of better durability and quieter operation. Look at the warranty period; a 2-year warranty generally signals a manufacturer confidence in their product longevity.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Established brands like Levoit, Blueair, and GermGuardian have extensive networks for replacement filters and customer support.
