## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Avanity Bathroom Fixtures And Materials

Choosing the right fixtures is a long-term investment in your home's value and your daily comfort. Before you make a purchase, consider these key factors to ensure you select the perfect Avanity products for your needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, measure your space carefully. A 73-inch double vanity won't fit in a 40-inch powder room. Note the location of your existing plumbing. Will it align with a new vanity, or will it need to be moved? Consider who will be using the bathroom. A busy master bathroom benefits from a double sink setup like the Avanity Adele 73", while a guest bathroom may only need a compact 31-inch vanity top. Think about storage needs—do you require lots of drawers for toiletries, or is open shelving more your style?

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Avanity products are known for their quality, which is reflected in the price. Set a realistic budget for your project. A simple quartz vanity top might range from $300 to $700 depending on size. A complete, high-end vanity set with a cabinet and top can range from $1,500 to over $4,000. Don't forget to budget for additional costs like a faucet, mirror, professional installation, and any necessary plumbing work. Investing in quality materials upfront often saves money on repairs and replacements in the long run.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that enhance functionality and longevity. For vanity tops, engineered quartz is a top choice due to its non-porous nature, making it resistant to stains, bacteria, and moisture. For cabinets, look for solid wood or plywood construction rather than particleboard for better durability in a humid environment. Features like soft-close drawers and doors prevent slamming and reduce wear and tear. Also, check the faucet spread (single-hole, 4-inch centerset, or 8-inch widespread) to ensure it matches the faucet you plan to buy.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Examine the details. A quality vanity cabinet will have dovetailed drawer joints and a multi-layer finish to protect against humidity. The thickness of a quartz top (typically 0.75 to 1.25 inches) can also indicate quality. Avanity is known for using durable materials like solid oak and resilient quartz, so you can be confident in the product's lifespan. Check warranties, as they reflect the manufacturer's confidence in their product's durability.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Choosing a reputable brand like Avanity provides peace of mind. Established brands have a track record of quality control, customer service, and consistent product availability. They are more likely to stand behind their products with solid warranties and provide support if any issues arise. Reading reviews and looking at customer photos can give you a real-world sense of the brand's performance and product quality.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Beyond just fitting in the room, consider the logistics of getting the fixture into your home. A large, one-piece 73-inch vanity is extremely heavy and may be difficult to maneuver through narrow hallways or up stairs. Some larger vanities come in modular pieces to make installation easier. For storage, think vertically. A taller cabinet or a medicine cabinet can provide extra storage without taking up more floor space. Ensure drawers can open fully without being obstructed by a door or other fixtures.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose avanity bathroom fixtures and materials?

Choosing the perfect fixtures can be a systematic process. Following a few best practices will ensure you make a choice you'll be happy with for years.

**1. Assess and Measure:** Start by accurately measuring your bathroom. Note the dimensions of the space where the vanity will go, including width, depth, and height constraints (like windows or light fixtures). Check the location of your plumbing supply lines and drain. This foundational step prevents the costly mistake of buying a piece that doesn't fit.

**2. Define Your Style:** Determine the aesthetic you want to achieve. Are you aiming for modern and minimalist, warm and traditional, or something in between? Collect inspiration from magazines or online platforms. Avanity offers a range of styles, from the clean lines of the Modero collection to the more classic appeal of other designs. Your style will guide your choice of finish, hardware, and countertop.

**3. Prioritize Materials:** For the bathroom, material choice is paramount. We strongly recommend engineered quartz for countertops due to its superior durability and low maintenance. For cabinets, solid wood or plywood are the best choices for longevity in a moist environment. These materials form the backbone of Avanity's quality reputation.

**4. Consider Functionality:** Think about how you use the space. Do you need maximum counter space? Ample concealed storage for a clutter-free look? A double sink for a shared bathroom? The Avanity Adele 73" set is ideal for couples, while a single-sink vanity like the 37" top on a custom base might be perfect for a primary suite.

**5. Avoid Common Mistakes:** A common error is overlooking the scale of fixtures in relation to the room. A massive vanity can overwhelm a small bathroom, while a tiny one can look lost in a large space. Another mistake is forgetting about the small details—faucet compatibility, hardware finish, and the inclusion of a backsplash. Always confirm these details before purchasing.

**6. Budget vs. Upgrade:** If you are on a tight budget, consider upgrading in phases. You could start with a high-quality vanity top like the Avanity 31" Quartz Top on your existing cabinet. If your budget allows, investing in a complete, integrated set provides the most cohesive and high-impact result. A premium all-in-one solution ensures that all components are designed to work together perfectly.
