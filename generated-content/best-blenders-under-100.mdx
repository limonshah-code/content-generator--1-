## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Blenders Under 100

Choosing the right blender in the under-$100 category requires a bit of knowledge. Here is what you should focus on to ensure you get a machine that meets your needs and provides lasting value.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, consider what you will be blending most often. Are you making daily protein shakes or fruit smoothies? A personal blender with 300-600 watts will be perfect. Do you want to crush ice for frozen cocktails, grind nuts, or blend hot soups? You will need a more powerful countertop model, likely in the 700-1000 watt range, with a durable, heat-resistant jar. Matching the blender's capabilities to your primary use is the most important step.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Within the under-$100 bracket, prices still vary. Personal, single-serve blenders typically fall in the $40-$70 range and are great for simple tasks. More robust countertop models with larger capacities and more power can be found from $70-$100. Set your budget based on your needs. Do not overspend on power and features you will never use, but avoid the cheapest options if you plan to blend tough ingredients regularly.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
