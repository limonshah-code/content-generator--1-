## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Cocktail Shaker For Beginners

### Find Your Need
Consider how you plan to use your shaker. If you are just starting and want to learn the basics, a simple 3-piece cobbler shaker with a built-in strainer is the easiest to handle. If you want to dive into a new hobby, a complete kit with a muddler and pourers will allow you to explore more complex recipes like Mojitos or Old Fashioneds. For those who prioritize hosting and aesthetics, a set with a storage stand is a great choice.

### Budget
Cocktail shakers for beginners typically range from $15 to $60.
- **$15-$25:** You will find reliable single-shakers and small 3-piece sets.
- **$30-$45:** This range includes comprehensive 7 to 15-piece kits with stands or insulated premium shakers.
- **$50+:** Expect high-end materials, triple insulation, or massive 20+ piece collections.

### Key Features
Look for a built-in strainer, as this saves you from needing to buy a separate Hawthorne strainer. A jigger cap is also a major plus for beginners, as it allows for precise measurements using the lid itself. Always opt for stainless steel, specifically 18/8 or 304 grade. These materials are food-safe, rust-resistant, and can withstand being dropped. Check the thickness; a 0.8 mm thickness is generally considered professional grade and will feel much more substantial and durable than thinner, cheaper alternatives.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose cocktail shaker for beginners?

Choosing the right shaker involves a few simple steps to ensure you get a tool that matches your skill level and interest.
1. **Start with a Cobbler Shaker:** For beginners, the 3-piece cobbler shaker (tin, built-in strainer, and cap) is almost always the best choice over a professional Boston shaker. It is much easier to seal and pour.
2. **Check the Seal:** Look for reviews or features mentioning leak-proof gaskets. A leaking shaker is the fastest way to ruin your bartending experience.
3. **Prioritize Stainless Steel:** Avoid plastic shakers as they do not chill the drink as effectively and can retain odors. Stick to 18/8 or 304 stainless steel.
4. **Consider the Extras:** If you don't own any bar tools, buy a set that includes a jigger and a mixing spoon. You cannot make balanced cocktails without accurate measurements.
5. **Read User Reviews on Ease of Opening:** A common mistake is buying a shaker that seals so tightly it becomes stuck. Look for models like OXO that are specifically designed to separate easily.
6. **Look for Dishwasher Safety:** If you hate cleaning, this is a non-negotiable feature. Most high-quality stainless steel models handle the dishwasher with ease.
