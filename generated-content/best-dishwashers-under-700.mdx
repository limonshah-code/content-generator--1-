## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Dishwashers Under 700

Choosing the right dishwasher involves more than just picking a brand. To ensure you get the best value and performance for your money, consider these crucial factors before making your purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess your household's needs. How large is your family? Do you cook and entertain often? This will determine the capacity you need. A standard 24-inch dishwasher holds around 12-14 place settings, which is suitable for most families. Larger families or avid entertainers might consider a 36-inch model, while couples or those in small apartments could benefit from a slimline 18-inch or a portable countertop model. Also, consider the types of dishes you wash most often. If you use many large pots, pans, or oddly shaped items, look for a dishwasher with adjustable racks and a spacious tub.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
While our guide focuses on models under $700, prices can still vary. Dishwashers in the $400-$550 range typically offer solid core performance with basic features, often with plastic tubs and fewer cycle options. As you move into the $550-$700 range, you'll find models with stainless steel tubs (which are more durable and better for drying), quieter operation (lower decibel ratings), and more advanced features like soil sensors, specialized wash zones, and smart controls. Set your budget based on the features that are most important to you.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that genuinely improve performance and convenience. A stainless steel tub is a must-have for durability, stain resistance, and better drying performance. Check the noise level, measured in decibels (dBA); anything under 50 dBA is considered quiet, while models under 45 dBA are whisper-quiet. An adjustable upper rack and fold-down tines add loading flexibility. Soil sensors that automatically adjust cycle time and water usage can save energy and improve cleaning results. Finally, look for an NSF-certified sanitize cycle, which uses high heat to kill 99.9% of bacteria.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The overall build quality will determine the lifespan of your dishwasher. Look for models with high-grade stainless steel interiors. Check the feel of the racks; they should slide smoothly without feeling flimsy. The door should feel solid and balanced when you open and close it. A reliable filtration system is also crucial for durability. Self-cleaning filters are convenient, but manual filters, especially multi-stage ones, often provide better performance and just need occasional rinsing.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While innovative new brands can offer great value, established manufacturers often have a proven track record of reliability and better customer service networks. Brands like Bosch, KitchenAid, Whirlpool, and Maytag are well-regarded in the dishwasher space. Do some research and read long-term reviews for any brand you're considering. A strong warranty (at least one year for parts and labor) and readily available replacement parts are also good indicators of a reputable brand.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your kitchen space carefully before buying. The standard dishwasher width is 24 inches, but you must account for depth and height as well. If you have non-standard cabinetry, a slimline 18-inch model might be necessary. For those who rent or lack the space for a built-in, a countertop model is an excellent portable solution. These units are compact, easy to install, and can be stored away when not in use, offering great flexibility.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose dishwashers under 700?

Choosing the right dishwasher can feel overwhelming, but a systematic approach makes it simple. Follow these steps to find the perfect model for your home and budget.

**1. Measure Your Space:** Before you do anything else, measure the height, width, and depth of the cutout where the dishwasher will go. A standard opening is 24 inches wide. Don't forget to measure the path to the kitchen to ensure it will fit through doorways.

**2. Define Your Must-Have Features:** Make a list of non-negotiable features. Is whisper-quiet operation (under 45 dBA) essential because your kitchen is open to the living area? Do you need a third rack for silverware and small items? Is a stainless steel tub a priority for you? Knowing your must-haves will help you filter your options quickly.

**3. Set Your Final Budget:** Decide on the maximum amount you're willing to spend, including potential costs for installation and a new installation kit (hoses, etc.). Sticking to a budget prevents you from overspending on features you may not need.

**4. Research and Compare Models:** Use our guide as a starting point. Compare the features, specs, and user reviews of your top 3-4 contenders. Pay close attention to long-term reliability reports and common complaints.

**5. Avoid Common Mistakes:** A common mistake is focusing only on looks. A stylish dishwasher that doesn't clean well is useless. Another mistake is assuming more cycles are always better; most households only use 2-3 cycles regularly. Finally, don't forget to check the drying performance. Models with heated dry options or those that use condensation drying with a stainless tub perform best.

**6. When to Upgrade vs. Budget:** If you simply need a machine that works, a basic model around $500 will do the job. However, if you value a quiet home, want better cleaning for tough messes, and desire the longevity of a stainless tub, it is well worth it to spend closer to the $700 mark. The upgrades in this range significantly improve the daily user experience.
