Before you purchase an essential oil, consider how you plan to use it. If you want to create an ambient, focused atmosphere in a room, a pure, undiluted oil blend for a diffuser is your best bet. If you need a quick mental boost at the office, during your commute, or at the library, a pre-diluted roll-on is far more convenient and practical. Think about your lifestyle: are you mostly stationary or constantly on the move? Your primary use case will guide you to the right format.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Essential oil prices can vary significantly. A simple, single oil like rosemary or a basic blend might cost between $7 and $15 for a 10ml bottle. More complex, premium blends with rare ingredients or larger bottle sizes (e.g., 30ml) can range from $15 to $30 or more. Roll-on applicators typically fall in the $10-$20 range. Set a realistic budget, but remember that with essential oils, price often reflects purity, sourcing quality, and the complexity of the blend.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
The most critical feature is purity. Look for labels that state '100% Pure Essential Oil' or 'Therapeutic Grade'. Check the ingredient list—a pure oil should only list the botanical names of the plants. For blends, see if the specific oils are listed. The extraction method, such as 'steam-distilled' or 'cold-pressed', also indicates quality. For roll-ons, check what carrier oil is used (e.g., Fractionated Coconut Oil, Jojoba Oil).

### Quality & Purity - What to look for
Quality goes beyond just the ingredients. Reputable brands often provide information about their sourcing and testing processes. Look for companies that perform third-party testing (like GC/MS testing) to verify the purity and composition of their oils. Packaging is also a quality indicator; essential oils should always be stored in dark-colored glass bottles (amber or cobalt blue) to protect them from light degradation, which can diminish their potency.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
An established brand with a long history of positive reviews is often a safer choice. Brands like Edens Garden have built a reputation for quality, transparency, and customer service over many years. Look for companies that are open about their sourcing, offer educational resources, and have accessible customer support, such as licensed aromatherapists on staff. A good reputation is a strong indicator of a reliable and effective product.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Consider the size of the bottle. Standard 10ml or 15ml bottles are great for home use and will last a considerable time in a diffuser. Larger 30ml bottles offer better value if you've found a blend you love and use frequently. For portability, nothing beats a 10ml roll-on. They are leak-proof, compact, and designed to be carried in a pocket, purse, or backpack for immediate use whenever you need it.
