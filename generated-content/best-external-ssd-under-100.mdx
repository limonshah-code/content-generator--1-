## What to Look for Before Buying the Best External Ssd Under 100

Choosing the right external SSD involves more than just picking the cheapest option. To find a drive that truly meets your needs, consider these critical factors.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about what you will use the drive for. Are you a gamer needing faster load times? A photographer backing up large RAW files? Or a student carrying assignments? Your use case determines the capacity and speed you require. Also, check compatibility. Ensure the drive’s connector (usually USB-C or USB-A) matches the ports on your computer, console, or tablet. While adapters exist, a native connection is always more reliable.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
While our focus is on drives under $100, prices within this range can still vary. You can typically find a quality 1TB SSD for around $70-$90, while a 500GB model might be closer to $50-$60.
