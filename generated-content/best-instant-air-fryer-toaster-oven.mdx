If you are primarily interested in replacing your toaster and making the occasional frozen pizza, a compact 4-slice or 6-slice model will suffice. However, if you intend to cook whole chickens or use the oven for family meal prep, you should look for units with at least a 10-quart or 19-quart capacity. Consider your typical portion sizes and the number of people you cook for regularly.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($100-$400)
The market for these appliances generally falls into three price tiers. The $100-$150 range typically offers reliable, compact units with 6-7 basic functions. In the $150-$250 range, you gain better build materials, more cooking functions (like dehydrate or rotisserie), and more powerful heating elements. Premium models over $300, like the Breville, offer advanced sensor technology, larger capacities, and superior durability. Setting a budget early helps you avoid paying for features you might never use.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for heating elements that provide even coverage. Quartz elements are generally preferred over metal coils because they respond faster and provide more intense heat. A high-speed convection fan is essential for the air fry function to work effectively. Other features to consider include digital vs. manual controls, the number of rack positions, and the availability of specialized modes like 'Dehydrate' or 'Proof.'

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Stainless steel interiors are often more durable and easier to clean than non-stick coatings, which can peel over time. Check the weight of the unit; a heavier oven often indicates better insulation and higher-quality components. Look for double-pane glass doors, which help retain heat and keep the exterior safe to touch. Brand reputation and warranty length (usually 1-2 years) are also strong indicators of a product's expected lifespan.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Buying from established brands like Instant Pot, Ninja, Cuisinart, or Breville ensures that you have access to customer support and replacement parts. These companies also provide extensive recipe databases and app support, which can be incredibly helpful for beginners. Established brands are also more likely to meet strict safety certifications like UL or ETL listing.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Always measure your counter space and the clearance under your cabinets before buying. Some air fryer ovens generate significant heat and require 4-6 inches of space on all sides for safety. If you have a tiny kitchen, look for space-saving designs like the Ninja Flip, which can be stored vertically when not in use. Remember that larger units are difficult to move, so they will likely become a permanent fixture on your counter.
