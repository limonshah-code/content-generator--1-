## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Irons For 90 Mph Swing Speed

Choosing the right equipment for a 90 mph swing speed involves more than just picking a brand. It's about matching technology and design to your specific swing characteristics to unlock your full potential.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, understand what you truly need. Is your current iron set holding you back? A 90 mph swing speed is right on the cusp between regular and stiff shafts. If your shots tend to fly high with a right-to-left curve (for a right-handed player) or feel uncontrolled, you might need a stiffer shaft. Conversely, if your ball flight is low and you struggle to get the ball airborne, a regular flex might be better. Also, consider if your goal is to *increase* your speed. In that case, a training aid like the SuperSpeed system might be a better investment than new irons.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Your budget will be a major factor. A single, high-quality graphite shaft like the KBS TGI can cost between $50 and $80. A complete speed training system can range from $100 for a value option like the HH-GOLF trainer to over $200 for a premium system like SuperSpeed Golf. A dedicated swing speed radar will typically cost between $150 and $250. It's important to set a realistic budget based on your goals. A small investment in the right training tool can often yield better results than a large investment in new clubs.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
For iron shafts, the most important features are **flex**, **weight**, and **kick point**. At 90 mph, a 'stiff-regular' or a 'firm' flex is often ideal, but it varies by manufacturer. Shaft weight (around 90-110 grams) can influence feel and control. For training aids, look for features like **adjustable weights**, **feedback mechanisms** (audible clicks or visual cues), and **app integration** for tracking progress. For a radar, **accuracy** and **ease of use** are paramount.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Look for equipment made from high-quality materials. Iron shafts should be made from high-modulus graphite or precision-engineered steel. Training aids should use durable materials like fiberglass or graphite for shafts and have secure weighting systems that won't come loose after repeated use. Read reviews to see how products hold up over time. A well-made tool will be a reliable part of your practice routine for years.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
