## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Lp Garage Heater With Thermostat

Choosing the right heater involves more than just picking the most powerful one. Consider these key factors to ensure you get a unit that perfectly matches your needs, space, and budget.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess your space. How large is your garage in square feet? Is it insulated? A well-insulated 500 sq. ft. garage needs far less heating power (BTUs) than a drafty 1,500 sq. ft. workshop. Also, consider your primary use. Are you just trying to keep the space above freezing to protect pipes, or do you need to maintain a comfortable 65°F for detailed woodworking? Your use case will determine whether you need a powerful, permanently installed unit or a smaller, portable heater.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
LP garage heaters come in a wide price range. Portable radiant heaters can start around $150-$250. Vent-free wall units typically fall in the $200-$400 range. For powerful, forced-air unit heaters that require venting and professional installation, expect to pay anywhere from $500 to $1,200 or more, not including the cost of installation. Set a realistic budget that accounts for the unit itself and any potential installation or accessory costs, like a thermostat or venting kit.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **BTU Output:** British Thermal Unit is the measure of heat output. A general rule of thumb is 30-40 BTUs per square foot for a well-insulated garage. Increase that number for poor insulation, high ceilings, or very cold climates.
- **Thermostat:** A built-in or compatible thermostat is non-negotiable for convenience and efficiency. It prevents the heater from running constantly, saving fuel and maintaining a stable temperature.
- **Safety Features:** Look for an Overheat Protection sensor, a Tip-Over Switch (for portable units), and a Low Oxygen Detection System (ODS) for vent-free models. These are critical for safe operation.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose lp garage heater with thermostat?

Choosing the best LP garage heater with a thermostat requires a methodical approach. Follow these steps to ensure you make the right choice for your specific needs.

**1. Calculate Your Heating Needs (BTUs):**
Start by measuring your garage's square footage (Length x Width). Then, use a BTU calculator online or follow this simple formula: (Square Footage x Ceiling Height x 0.133) x Temperature Rise = Required BTUs. 'Temperature Rise' is the difference between the coldest outside temperature and your desired inside temperature. This gives you a baseline for the power you need. Always err on the side of slightly more power, especially for poorly insulated spaces.

**2. Select the Right Type of Heater:**
- **Forced-Air Unit Heaters:** Best for large, open spaces. They mount to the ceiling, save floor space, and distribute heat evenly. They require venting to the outside.
- **Vent-Free Heaters (Blue Flame/Infrared):** Best for well-insulated, smaller garages. They are easy to install but require adequate ventilation in the room itself for safety.
- **Portable Radiant Heaters:** Best for targeted spot heating or for users who need flexibility and mobility. They heat objects directly and are great for job sites.

**3. Prioritize Critical Safety Features:**
Do not compromise on safety. For any type of heater, look for automatic overheat shut-off. For portable units, a tip-over switch is essential. For vent-free models, a low-oxygen sensor (ODS) is a mandatory feature that shuts the unit off if oxygen levels in the room drop.

**4. Consider Installation and Venting Requirements:**
Be realistic about the installation. A portable heater is ready to go out of the box. A vent-free wall heater is a relatively simple DIY project. A large, forced-air unit heater, however, typically requires professional installation for running gas lines and venting through a roof or wall, which adds to the total cost.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- **Under-sizing the Heater:** Buying a heater that's too small for your space will cause it to run constantly without ever reaching the desired temperature, wasting fuel and causing excessive wear.
- **Ignoring Insulation:** The best heater in the world will struggle in a garage with massive air gaps. Sealing drafts around doors and windows is a cost-effective first step to improve any heater's performance.
- **Forgetting Clearances:** Every heater has specified minimum clearances from walls, ceilings, and flammable materials. Ignoring these is a major fire hazard.
