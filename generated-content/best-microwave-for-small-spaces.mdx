## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Microwave For Small Spaces

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase a compact microwave, think about your primary use case. Are you mostly reheating coffee and frozen dinners, or do you plan to defrost meat and steam vegetables? If you only need it for light tasks, a 700W model is perfect. However, if you cook full meals, you might prefer a 900W or 1000W model that fits in a similar footprint. Also, measure your counter space and cabinet clearance carefully, leaving at least an inch for ventilation.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Small microwaves are generally affordable. You can find reliable entry-level models between $60 and $90. These usually offer 700W and basic features. If you move into the $100 to $150 range, you will see higher wattage (900W+), sensor cooking, and premium finishes like black stainless steel. Setting a budget early will help you decide if features like a mute button or smart sensor are worth the extra investment.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for features that enhance usability. A mute button is excellent for late-night use, while a child lock is essential for safety. Check the turntable size; a 10-inch or larger tray is necessary if you use standard dinner plates. One-touch express buttons (1-6 minutes) are massive time-savers. Lastly, check if it has a weight defrost option, as it is much more accurate than simple time defrosting.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
In a small microwave, the door latch is often the first thing to fail. Look for models with a sturdy push-button or a high-quality pull handle like the Galanz Retro. Check user reviews for mentions of the turntable motor noise, as a grinding sound can indicate lower-quality components. Brands like Toshiba and Farberware are known for using more durable internal components even in their smaller units.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy microwave for small spaces?

Investing in a microwave specifically designed for small spaces is a very smart decision for several reasons. First and foremost is the efficient use of your kitchen footprint. Compared to traditional full-sized microwaves, these compact units are also significantly more energy-efficient. ## What Is the Best Way to Choose microwave for small spaces?

To choose the best small microwave, you should follow a systematic approach. Start by measuring the exact dimensions of where the microwave will sit. Next, prioritize your must-have features. If you hate the loud beeping sound that most microwaves make, only look at models with a mute function (most Toshiba and Chefman models have this). If you have young children, ensure the child lock is easy for you to activate. Once you have a shortlist, check the wattage. If you are used to a powerful office microwave, a 700W home model might feel slow. If speed is your priority, look for the 900W Toshiba 0.9 cu ft models.
