## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Neutral Paint Colors For Rental Property

## What Is the Best Way to Choose neutral paint colors for rental property?

Choosing the right neutral isn't just about picking a random beige. Follow these steps for a professional result:

1.  **Assess the Lighting:** Look at the room during different times of day. Warm neutrals (with yellow or red undertones) work well in north-facing rooms with cool light, while cool neutrals (with blue or gray undertones) balance out the intense sun in south-facing rooms.
2.  **Check the Floor:** Your paint should complement the flooring. If you have gray-toned carpets, stick to cool neutrals. If you have warm hardwood, go for warmer tones like Whispering Wheat or Country Tan.
3.  **Test the Coverage:** Apply a small sample to the wall to see how many coats it takes to cover the existing color. High-quality paints like those from Martha Stewart will usually require fewer coats.
4.  **Consider the Finish:** Use matte for ceilings and low-traffic areas to hide bumps. Use satin or semi-gloss for kitchens, bathrooms, and baseboards where moisture and scuffs are common.
5.  **Avoid Pure White:** Stark white can feel clinical and shows every speck of dirt. Opt for "off-white" or "linen" to get the brightness of white with a bit more forgiveness.
6.  **Think Long-Term:** Pick a color that the manufacturer has produced for years. This ensures that when you need a touch-up in 2027, the color will still be available on the shelf.
