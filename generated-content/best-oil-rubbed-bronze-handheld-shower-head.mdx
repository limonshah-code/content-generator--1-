## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Oil Rubbed Bronze Handheld Shower Head

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase, assess your current shower setup. Most handheld units use a standard 1/2-inch NPT connection, making them compatible with almost all US shower arms. However, you should consider who will be using the shower. If you have children or pets, a longer hose (6 feet or more) and a unit with a pause button are invaluable. You can find oil rubbed bronze shower heads at various price points. Budget models ($25-$45) are typically made of high-grade ABS plastic; they look great and perform well but may not last decades. ### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for the number and type of spray settings. Common modes include rain, massage, mist, and combinations of these. A pause mode is a huge plus for water conservation. Additionally, check the face size; a larger face (4-7 inches) provides more coverage but may require more water pressure to feel effective. Hose material is also critical—stainless steel is generally more flexible and durable than PVC.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The finish is what you see, but the internal components determine how long it lasts. Solid brass or metal diverters and hose nuts are signs of high quality. For the spray head itself, high-grade ABS is standard and resistant to rust, but solid metal is the gold standard for durability. Look for 'rub-clean' or silicone nozzles which allow you to easily remove mineral buildup with your thumb, preventing clogs that can cause internal pressure to build and damage the unit.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Buying from established brands like Delta, AquaDance, or HammerHead often guarantees better quality control and customer support. These brands typically offer limited lifetime warranties, which are essential because shower heads are subject to constant water pressure and mineral exposure. In smaller shower stalls, a massive dual rainfall combo might feel cramped. Conversely, in a large master walk-in, a tiny 3-inch head might look out of place. Consider the 'parking' height of the handheld unit; some brackets sit high on the shower arm, while others include wall-mount options for lower placement. Make sure the hose is long enough to reach all corners of your tub if you plan on using it for cleaning.
