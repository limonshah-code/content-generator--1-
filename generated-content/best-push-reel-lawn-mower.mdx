## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Push Reel Lawn Mower

Choosing the right push reel mower involves considering your lawn's specific characteristics and your personal preferences. Here’s a breakdown of the key factors to help you make the best decision.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, assess your lawn. How large is it? A wider cutting deck (16-18 inches) is better for larger areas to reduce mowing time, while a smaller deck (14 inches) is more maneuverable for small, complex yards with obstacles. Also, consider your grass type. Most reel mowers work well on common grasses, but dense, tough grasses like Bermuda or Zoysia benefit from mowers with more blades (a 7-blade reel versus the standard 4 or 5). Finally, think about terrain. Reel mowers perform best on relatively flat, smooth lawns.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Push reel mowers are generally very affordable compared to their gas and electric counterparts. You can find excellent entry-level models for around $70-$100. Mid-range mowers with better features like more blades or wider decks typically fall in the $100-$150 range. Specialized or premium models, such as those with 7 blades, can cost upwards of $150. Set a budget based on your lawn size and desired features, but know that even the most affordable options can provide great results.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
- **Cutting Width:** Determines how much grass is cut in one pass. Wider means faster mowing on open lawns; narrower means better agility.
- **Blade Count:** Most have 4 or 5 blades, which is great for general-purpose mowing. A 7-blade reel provides a finer, more precise cut for dense, low-growing grasses.
- **Cutting Height Adjustment:** Look for a mower with a good range of height settings (e.g., 1 to 3 inches). This allows you to adapt to different seasons and grass conditions.
- **Wheel Size:** Larger wheels (10 inches) roll more easily over bumpy or uneven ground, making the mower easier to push.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The most critical component is the blades. Look for models with blades made of heat-treated or hardened alloy steel, as they will hold their edge longer. A sturdy frame and durable wheels (often made of composite polymer) are also important for longevity. A mower that feels solid and well-constructed will provide years of reliable service with minimal maintenance.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Sticking with reputable brands like American Lawn Mower Company, Great States, LawnMaster, and Earthwise is a good strategy. These companies have a long history of producing reliable reel mowers and generally offer better customer support and easier access to replacement parts should you ever need them.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
One of the great advantages of reel mowers is their compact size. They are much smaller and lighter than gas mowers, making them easy to store in a crowded garage or small shed. Consider the mower's weight if you need to carry it up steps or hang it on a wall. Most models are under 30 pounds, making them very manageable for almost anyone.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy push reel lawn mower?

Investing in a push reel lawn mower is an exceptionally wise decision for many homeowners, particularly those with small to medium-sized lawns. The primary value proposition lies in the health of your lawn. Unlike rotary mowers that tear and shred grass blades, a reel mower acts like a pair of scissors, making a clean, precise cut. This clean cut helps the grass heal faster, reduces its susceptibility to disease, and promotes healthier, lusher growth. This single benefit can transform the look and feel of your turf over time.

Beyond lawn health, the long-term benefits are significant. There are no ongoing costs for gas, oil, spark plugs, or expensive engine repairs. The only maintenance required is occasional cleaning and sharpening of the blades. This makes the total cost of ownership incredibly low. Furthermore, the environmental benefits are undeniable. Reel mowers produce zero emissions and operate almost silently, allowing you to mow early in the morning or late in the evening without disturbing your family or neighbors. While it does require physical effort, many users find it to be a pleasant form of light exercise. For the cost-conscious, eco-aware homeowner who values a pristine lawn, a push reel mower is not just a tool; it's a smart, sustainable investment.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose push reel lawn mower?

Choosing the best push reel mower is a straightforward process when you approach it systematically. Follow these steps to ensure you select the perfect model for your needs.

**1. Assess Your Lawn:** Start by measuring your lawn's approximate size. For yards under 2,000 sq. ft., a 14 or 16-inch mower is ideal. For larger yards, consider an 18-inch model to save time. Identify your grass type. If you have a tough, dense turf like Zoysia, prioritize a 7-blade mower. For most other grasses, a 5-blade mower is excellent.

**2. Set Your Budget:** Decide how much you're comfortable spending. You can get a solid, functional mower for under $100, but investing a little more ($100-$150) often gets you better durability, a wider cut, or more convenience features like a grass catcher.

**3. Compare Key Features:** Focus on cutting width, number of blades, and height adjustment range. Ensure the height range matches your desired lawn length. Check reviews for comments on ease of assembly and use.

**4. Avoid Common Mistakes:** A frequent error is buying a mower that’s too small for a large lawn, which leads to frustration. Another is neglecting to keep the blades sharp; dull blades will tear the grass, defeating the purpose of a reel mower. Finally, don't expect a reel mower to easily handle a jungle—they are designed for regular maintenance, not for clearing severely overgrown areas.

**5. When to Upgrade:** If you're using a basic 4-blade mower and aren't satisfied with the cut quality, upgrading to a 5-blade or 7-blade model will make a noticeable difference. If you're tired of raking, choosing a model with an included grass catcher is a worthwhile upgrade for convenience.

**What features are most important in push reel lawn mower?**
The most important features are the cutting width, the number of blades, and the adjustable cutting height. The cutting width affects how quickly you can mow, the blade count determines the quality of the cut (more is finer), and the height adjustment allows you to adapt to your lawn's needs throughout the year.

**How do I maintain my push reel lawn mower?**
Maintenance is simple. After each use, wipe down the blades to remove grass clippings and moisture to prevent rust. Once a year, or as needed, you should sharpen the blades using a lapping kit. You can also lightly oil the moving parts to keep them operating smoothly. That's it—no gas, oil, or filters to worry about.

**Are expensive push reel lawn mower worth it?**
For most people, a mid-range mower offers the best value. However, a more expensive model can be worth it if you have specific needs. For example, if you have dense Bermuda grass, a pricier 7-blade mower will provide a significantly better cut than a standard 5-blade model, making the extra cost a worthwhile investment for a superior lawn.

**How long do push reel lawn mower typically last?**
With proper care, a push reel mower can last for many years, often a decade or more. Because there is no engine, the primary components are the steel frame, blades, and wheels. As long as you keep the blades sharp and clean the mower after use to prevent rust, it will remain a reliable tool for a very long time.
