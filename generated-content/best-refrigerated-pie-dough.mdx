## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Refrigerated Pie Dough

Choosing the right pie dough can elevate your baking from good to unforgettable. Before you grab the first box you see, consider these key factors to ensure you're getting the best product for your specific needs.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about what you're making. Are you baking a classic double-crust apple pie? Then a product like the [Pillsbury Premade Pie Crusts](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B003D1Z2E2?tag=home-essentials-guide-20&linkCode=osi&th=1&psc=1) with two crusts is ideal. If you're making a savory quiche or a single-crust pumpkin pie, you'll have an extra crust for later. For free-form galettes, pastries, or appetizers, a seamless dough sheet like the [Pillsbury Crescent Dough Sheet](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B005FNVH04?tag=home-essentials-guide-20&linkCode=osi&th=1&psc=1) offers more flexibility. For those with dietary needs, a specialty product like the [Wholly Wholesome Organic Pie Dough](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B077ZJX656?tag=home-essentials-guide-20&linkCode=osi&th=1&psc=1) is the perfect fit.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Refrigerated pie dough and mixes are generally affordable, but prices can vary. Expect to pay between $3 and $6 for a standard refrigerated pie dough package. Specialty organic or vegan options will be on the higher end of that range, typically $6 to $9. ## What Is the Best Way to Choose refrigerated pie dough?

Selecting the perfect refrigerated pie dough is a simple process if you follow a few key steps. By thinking through your needs and understanding the options, you can ensure a delicious outcome for your baking projects.

**1. Assess Your Recipe:** First, determine what you are baking. A classic covered apple pie needs a two-crust pack. A quiche or pumpkin pie only needs one. For something unconventional like a galette, a dough sheet is best. This initial step narrows down your options immediately.

**2. Check the Ingredients:** Take a moment to read the label. If you prefer natural ingredients, look for doughs without hydrogenated oils, artificial flavors, or preservatives. If you have dietary needs, seek out certified organic, vegan, or gluten-free options. The ingredients directly impact the flavor and health profile of your final product.

**3. Consider Ease of Use:** Most refrigerated doughs are very easy to use, but some can be more fragile than others. Read user reviews to see if people report issues with cracking or tearing. A good dough should be pliable and forgiving.

**4. Don't Forget the Freezer Aisle:** While our focus is refrigerated dough, don't completely discount frozen options. They have a much longer shelf life and are great to keep on hand for baking emergencies. The main drawback is the need to thaw them properly before use.

**5. Common Mistakes to Avoid:** A frequent mistake is trying to unroll the dough straight from the fridge. Let it sit on the counter for about 15 minutes to become more pliable. Another error is overworking the dough when patching tears; handle it as little as possible to keep it tender. Finally, for wet fillings, always consider blind-baking the crust partially to prevent a soggy bottom.
