## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Sectionals For Small Spaces Small Space Sectionals

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you hit the buy button, measure your space at least twice. Consider where the chaise will go; if you move frequently, a reversible or modular design is essential so the sofa can adapt to different room layouts. Think about how you use your living room—if you frequently have guests, a model with a movable ottoman for extra seating is better than a fixed chaise.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
You can find quality small-space sectionals in the $300 to $800 range. Options at the lower end ($300-$450) usually feature linen fabric and foam cushions, while mid-range models ($500-$800) often include hardwood frames, inner springs, or specialized fabrics like chenille and corduroy. Setting a realistic budget helps you prioritize whether you want better fabric or extra features like hidden storage.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for high-density foam (at least 1.8 lbs/cu ft) or inner springs to ensure the couch doesn't sag within a year. Reversible chaises, storage ottomans, and side pockets are the most valuable features for small-space living. Also, check the assembly type; tool-free assembly is a major convenience for those living in lofts or walk-up apartments.
