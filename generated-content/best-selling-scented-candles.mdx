## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Selling Scented Candles

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before purchasing, consider where the candle will be placed. Large jars with single wicks are excellent for long, slow burns in living rooms, while smaller 9oz jars or multi-packs are better suited for bathrooms or bedrooms. If you want to mask kitchen odors, look for citrus or herbal notes. For relaxation, lavender and vanilla are your best bets.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($15-$45 ranges)
Budget candles usually range from $15 to $20 and offer great value with long burn times, like the Yankee large jars. Mid-range boutique candles often cost $20 to $30 and focus on style and hand-poured quality. Luxury candles like NEST or Capri Blue can exceed $40, offering complex fragrance layers and designer vessels that serve as home decor.

### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for wax type and wick material. Soy wax and plant wax blends are preferred over paraffin as they tend to burn cleaner with less soot. Lead-free cotton wicks are a must for safety. ### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
High-quality candles should have a centered wick to prevent uneven melting or tunneling. The glass vessel should be thick enough to withstand heat for several hours. A good candle will also maintain its scent from the first burn until the bottom of the jar, rather than losing its aroma halfway through.

### 5. Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Established brands like Yankee Candle and NEST New York have rigorous testing processes to ensure safety and fragrance consistency. Buying from reputable brands often means you have access to better customer support and a guarantee that the ingredients listed are what is actually in the candle.

### 6. Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
If you travel or want to switch scents frequently, smaller jars or travel tins are ideal. Large 22oz jars are heavy and meant to stay in one place. Consider the footprint of the jar on your nightstand or shelf—some decorative jars like the Capri Blue Volcano have a wider base that requires more surface area.
