## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Weslock Keyed Door Knobs

Choosing the right door knob is about more than just looks; it's about security, functionality, and longevity. Before you make a purchase, consider these key factors to ensure you select the perfect hardware for your home.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, determine where the knob will be used. An exterior front door requires a keyed entry knob with a deadbolt for high security. A back door or garage entry also needs a keyed lock. For interior doors like bedrooms or bathrooms, a privacy knob (with a lock but no key) is sufficient. For closets, a non-locking passage knob or a non-turning dummy knob is appropriate. Also, measure your door's thickness and the 'backset' (the distance from the edge of the door to the center of the borehole) to ensure compatibility. Standard backsets are 2-3/8" or 2-3/4".

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($25-$250 ranges)
Door knob prices can vary dramatically. A basic, budget-friendly keyed knob from a brand like Copper Creek might cost between $25 and $40. Mid-range, high-quality options from brands like Weslock or Schlage, offering better security (ANSI Grade 2) and more stylish designs, typically fall in the $50 to $150 range. For premium designer handlesets with advanced features like panic-proof egress or smart capabilities, you can expect to pay anywhere from $150 to $250 or more. Set a budget based on your security needs and aesthetic desires.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for the ANSI (American National Standards Institute) grade. Grade 3 is standard residential security, Grade 2 is better for enhanced security, and Grade 1 is top-tier commercial security. Check if the knob is reversible for left- and right-handed doors. An adjustable latch is a convenient feature that fits multiple backset sizes. For handlesets, features like a panic-proof function, which unlocks the deadbolt and latch in one motion, can be a valuable safety addition.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The material of the knob is a key indicator of its durability. Look for knobs made from solid brass, bronze, or zinc alloy. These materials are resistant to corrosion and can withstand heavy use. The quality of the finish is also important. A good finish, like PVD (Physical Vapor Deposition), will resist tarnishing, scratching, and fading over time. A solid, heavy feel is often a good sign of quality internal components.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Established brands like Weslock, Schlage, and Kwikset have built reputations for reliability and quality. They often back their products with robust warranties, such as a limited lifetime mechanical and finish warranty. Choosing a reputable brand ensures you're getting a product that has been tested and is supported by customer service in case you run into any issues. While newer or budget brands can offer good value, established manufacturers provide an extra layer of confidence.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
While not 'portable' in the traditional sense, the size and style of the knob should match the scale of your door and home. A large, ornate handleset might overwhelm a small, simple door, while a tiny knob could look out of place on a grand entryway. Consider the projection of the knob (how far it sticks out from the door) to ensure it doesn't interfere with storm doors or wall obstructions.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose weslock keyed door knobs?

Choosing the right Weslock keyed door knob involves a few simple steps to ensure you get the perfect match for your home's security and style.

**1. Assess Your Security Needs:**
First, determine the level of security required. For a front door or any primary exterior entry, a handleset with a separate deadbolt or a knob that meets ANSI Grade 2 standards is highly recommended. For a less critical entry point, a standard keyed knob might suffice.

**2. Measure Your Door:**
Before you shop, take two crucial measurements: the door's thickness (typically 1-3/8" to 1-3/4" for interior, and up to 2-1/4" for exterior) and the backset (the distance from the door's edge to the center of the lock hole, usually 2-3/8" or 2-3/4"). Most Weslock products have adjustable backsets, but it's always best to confirm.

**3. Match Your Home's Aesthetic:**
Weslock offers a wide range of styles and finishes. Consider your home's architectural style. A modern home might suit a Xanthis handleset in matte black, while a traditional colonial home would be perfectly complemented by an antique brass Lexington set. Choose a finish (e.g., satin nickel, oil-rubbed bronze, matte black) that coordinates with your exterior light fixtures, house numbers, and overall color scheme.

**4. Consider Special Features:**
Do you need any special functionality? For families, a panic-proof handleset can provide extra safety by allowing for quick exits. If you're matching hardware throughout the house, make sure to look at the corresponding passage, privacy, and dummy knobs within the same product family.

**5. Avoid Common Mistakes:**
A common mistake is buying only based on looks and ignoring the security grade. Another is forgetting to check your door's measurements, leading to compatibility issues. Don't assume all knobs fit all doors. Finally, avoid choosing a finish that clashes with your existing exterior hardware; consistency is key to a polished look.

By following these steps, you can confidently select a Weslock keyed door knob that provides the right level of security, fits your door perfectly, and enhances your home's curb appeal.
