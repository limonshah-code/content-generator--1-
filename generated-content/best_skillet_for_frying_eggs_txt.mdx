## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Skillet For Frying Eggs

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you choose a pan, identify how many eggs you typically cook at once. An 8-inch pan is perfect for one or two eggs, while a 12-inch pan is better for large family omelets. You must also check your stovetop type; while most modern pans are induction-compatible, some professional aluminum pans only work on gas or electric.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($15-$60 ranges)
Setting a realistic budget helps narrow down your choices quickly. Budget-friendly options in the $15-$25 range usually offer excellent granite or ceramic coatings that last a year or two. Premium options in the $40-$60 range, like All-Clad or Tramontina, offer better heat retention and significantly longer lifespans, often backed by lifetime warranties.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look for non-stick surfaces that specifically mention being PFOA and PFAS free. A thick base is also vital to prevent warping and ensure even heat distribution. If you enjoy finishing your eggs or frittatas in the oven, pay close attention to the oven-safe temperature rating, which can vary from 350 to over 800 degrees.
